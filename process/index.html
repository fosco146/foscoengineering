How are ETAP, SKM PowerTools, and EasyPower models verified during a third-party review?

We verify engineering simulation models by extracting the raw native files (.PRW for SKM, .OTW for ETAP) and independently auditing the underlying software database assumptions. Rather than just reviewing the final output reports, our review explicitly cross-references the component manager settings against manufacturer datasheets to catch over-conservative or dangerously miscalculated boundary conditions.

How does a third-party engineering review validate available fault current data?

We audit the model’s incoming utility source data to ensure the short-circuit calculations are structurally sound. We cross-reference the software's utility component configuration against official, dated written documentation from the local electrical utility provider, verifying that the maximum and minimum available short-circuit currents and X/R ratios reflect the actual physical point of common coupling rather than a generic infinite bus assumption.

How does a peer review evaluate circuit breaker clearing times and TCC curves?

We evaluate clearing times by plotting custom Time-Current Characteristic (TCC) curves for every upstream protective device and main breaker within the model. Our review verifies that the original engineer did not blindly default to the arbitrary IEEE 1584 2-second clearing time rule on main lugs without confirming that real-world physical containment, relay settings, and maintenance switch options (such as ERMS) support safe worker evacuation.
