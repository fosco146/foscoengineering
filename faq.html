General

What is an arc flash study, and why does my facility need one?
An arc flash study analyzes your electrical distribution system to determine the incident energy and required personal protective equipment (PPE) at each piece of equipment. It identifies the potential severity of an arc flash event so equipment can be properly labeled and personnel can work safely. OSHA requires employers to protect workers from electrical hazards, and NFPA 70E is the industry-recognized standard for meeting that obligation, making an arc flash study a practical requirement for any facility with employees working on or near energized equipment.
What is included in a third-party engineering review?
A third-party review is an independent audit of engineering work performed by another firm, such as an arc flash study, short circuit analysis, or coordination study. It checks the model inputs, assumptions, calculation methods, and conclusions against the applicable standards to confirm the work is accurate and defensible. This is commonly requested for quality control, peer review requirements, litigation support, or simply as a second opinion before relying on safety-critical results.

Arc Flash & Power System Studies

How often does an arc flash study need to be updated?
NFPA 70E recommends reviewing arc flash studies at least every five years to account for changes in equipment, utility fault current, or facility load. A study should also be updated sooner whenever there is a significant change to the electrical system, such as new equipment, a service upgrade, or a change in utility transformer size, since any of these can change incident energy levels and invalidate existing labels.
What is the difference between an arc flash study and a short circuit study?
A short circuit study calculates the available fault current at each point in your electrical system, which is used to verify that equipment is rated to safely interrupt or withstand a fault. An arc flash study builds on those results, along with protective device coordination data, to calculate incident energy and determine the required PPE category. In practice, the two are typically performed together as part of the same overall power system study.
What standards do you follow for arc flash and power system studies?
Studies are performed in accordance with NFPA 70E for electrical safety in the workplace, IEEE 1584 for arc flash hazard calculations, and the National Electrical Code (NEC) for system design requirements. All modeling and analysis is performed using industry-standard software.
What information do you need to start a study?
At minimum, we need one-line diagrams, equipment nameplate data (transformers, breakers, cables, and protective devices), and available utility fault current data. If as-built documentation is incomplete, we can coordinate field data collection to verify equipment in place before the study begins.
Do you provide arc flash labels for equipment?
Yes. Every study includes NFPA 70E-compliant arc flash labels showing incident energy, PPE category, and boundary distances for each piece of equipment analyzed, ready to print and apply in the field.
How long does a typical arc flash study take?
Timeline depends on facility size and the completeness of existing documentation, but most studies are completed within a few weeks of receiving full system data. Projects with incomplete documentation or that require field data collection take longer. Contact us with your project details for a specific estimate.

Third-Party Review

When should I get a third-party review of an existing study?
A third-party review makes sense any time you need independent verification of engineering work: before relying on safety-critical labels, when quality varies across firms and you want assurance, as part of a formal peer review or QC process, or when a study is being challenged or scrutinized. It is also useful when taking over a facility or portfolio and inheriting studies performed by an unknown firm.
Will you work directly with the firm that did the original study?
We work directly with you, the client. Depending on the scope, that can include coordinating with the original engineering firm to obtain source files and clarify assumptions, but our review and findings are delivered independently to protect the integrity of the assessment.
